Two Powerful Core Features

Docsie gives you two equally important tools in one clean interface — one to replace, one to find. Both work across .docx, .xlsx, .txt, and .csv files.

Feature #1 — Bulk Word Replacer

Point Docsie at a folder. Tell it what word to find and what to replace it with. Click one button. Docsie opens every document — Word files, Excel spreadsheets, text files, CSVs — and replaces every instance. Across 10 files or 1,000.

✦ Live Preview

See every proposed change — file by file, word by word — before a single character is modified. Approve or cancel with one click.

✦ Automatic Backup

Saves pristine copies of original files to a "_backups" folder before writing any changes. Your originals are always safe.

✦ Similarity Threshold Slider

Catches typos and spelling variants automatically. "Centre" vs "Center," "Johanson" vs "Johansson" — all caught by fuzzy matching (10%–100%).

✦ Whole Word Mode

Prevents "it" from matching inside "written." ON by default — protects you from destructive partial matches.

Feature #2 — Smart Word Finder

A read-only scanning engine that finds words across all your documents in seconds — without modifying anything. Think of it as a forensic audit for your files. You're not guessing anymore. You KNOW.

✦ Deep Scan

Dives into every subfolder, recursively. Scan an entire project directory — 500 files across 20 subfolders — in seconds.

✦ Similarity Threshold Slider

Finds words that LOOK like your word. Search for "Johnson" and find "Johnsen (90%)", "Johanson (85%)" — every shadow of your search term.

✦ Clean Results

Only shows files with matches — no noise. Exact counts, similarity percentages, and relative file paths for every hit.

✦ 100% Read-Only

Your files are never written to during a search. Zero risk. Open, scan, close, report.

I Didn't Want to Build Software. I Just Wanted My Weekend Back.

The reluctant origin story of Docsie

I'm a 20-year-old developer. I never planned to build a product. I was just trying to survive a corporate job that had me drowning in documents — Hundreds of files that needed the same word changed after a client rebranded.

I spent an entire weekend — Saturday morning to Sunday night — doing nothing but opening files, pressing Ctrl+H, and praying I didn't miss one. By the time I was done, I had a headache, bloodshot eyes, and the sinking feeling that I'd definitely missed something.

That Monday, I wrote a quick Python script. Not because I wanted to start a company. Because I refused to ever lose another weekend to something that stupid. What I built that day became Docsie.

The first time I ran it on a real project — Hundreds of documents, four file types, nested subfolders — the script finished in under 8 seconds. I literally laughed. The same job that stole my entire weekend was done before I could take a sip of coffee.

I realized I couldn't be the only one suffering through this. There had to be thousands of people — legal assistants, writers, admins, accountants — losing hours to the same mindless, soul-crushing manual work. So I polished it, added a proper interface, and made it available to anyone who needed it.

"I didn't set out to build a tool. I set out to save my weekend. Docsie is the result of refusing to do the same boring task twice."

— The reluctant developer behind Docsie
